# Baby Duck Stories
*Three original stories for a little one who loves being Baby Duck*

---

## Story 1: Baby Duck and the Very Big Hill
*[Confidence]*

Baby duck and Taytay duck went for a walk one morning.
The sun was warm. The sky was blue.
And then — baby duck stopped.

There was a hill.
A very, very big hill.

Baby duck looked at the hill. The hill looked back.
"It's too tall!" said baby duck. "What if I fall and SPLAT?"

Taytay duck knelt right down.
"You won't SPLAT," said Taytay duck. "Your two little feet are VERY good at walking. And I'll walk right beside you. Every step."

Baby duck looked at his feet. They were small. But they were his.

"Okay," said baby duck. "One step."

*One step.*

The grass was soft. The sun was warm.

"Another step," said baby duck.

*Another step.*

The hill was still tall. But baby duck was getting closer.

*Another step. Another step. Another.*

And then —

Baby duck was at the TOP.

Whoa.

From up there, the houses looked like toys. The cars looked like beetles. Taytay duck looked like... the best dad in the whole wide world.

"I did it!" said baby duck. "Taytay, I DID IT!"

Taytay duck scooped him up and spun him around.

"You were SO BRAVE," said Taytay duck. "You were scared and you did it ANYWAY."

Baby duck thought about that.

He was scared.
And he did it anyway.

*That's what brave means.*

And then they walked down the hill together — which was way easier than going up — and baby duck said:

"Can we do the hill again TOMORROW?"

## Story 2: Baby Duck and the Great Splash
*[Playfulness]*

One rainy morning, baby duck and Taytay duck could not go outside.

The rain came down. Pitter. Patter. PITTER. PATT.
The sky was gray. The yard was wet.

"I want to GO!" said baby duck.

"Nope," said Taytay duck. "But I have an idea..."

Bubbles! A towel! And the bathtub!

Baby duck's eyes went WIDE.

The bath was ready. The bubbles were BIG. And taytay duck brought in all the rubber ducks — yellow ones, red ones, a duck with a hat, and the tiny one that fit right in baby duck's hand.

"Okay ducklings," said taytay duck. "It's splash time."

SPLASH!

Baby duck kicked his feet and the water went EVERYWHERE. All over taytay duck. All over the mirror. All over the ducks.

"WARNING!" shouted baby duck. "FLOOD IN THE BATHROOM!"

Taytay duck splashed back.

The duck with the hat went under. The tiny duck went flying. The big yellow duck did a TWIRL.

They laughed and laughed until their bellies hurt.

When the bath was done, taytay duck wrapped baby duck in the big towel and rubbed his head until he was a fluffy puffball.

"Same time tomorrow?" asked baby duck.

Taytay duck looked at the soaked bathroom, the dripping mirror, the drowned ducks.

"Same time tomorrow," said taytay duck.

## Story 3: The Storm and the Brave Duckling
*[Strength]*

That night, there was a storm.

THUNDER roared outside. The rain went BAM BAM BAM on the window.

Baby duck woke up. His room was dark. His heart went DUB-DUB-DUB.

"BABY DUCK?!" he shouted. "TAYTAY?!"

Taytay duck came running. His feet went pat-pat-pat-pat on the floor. He scooped up baby duck and held him tight.

"It's okay, little one. I'm here. I'm right here."

"The NOISE!" said baby duck. "It's SO LOUD!"

"I know," said taytay duck. "It's just the sky having a tantrum. It happens sometimes."

"Is it... is it going to come GET us?"

Taytay duck thought.

"Nope," he said. "The house is your shield. The walls are your armor. Nothing in that storm can reach you in here."

Baby duck looked around. His room. His blanket. His lamp. His dad.

"But taytay... what if it's still scared?"

Taytay duck smiled.

"Then we can be scared together. And we can still open the door and look at it. Want to?"

Baby duck held on tight. But he nodded.

Taytay duck carried him to the front door. They opened it together.

WHOOOOSH.

The wind was wild. It pulled at their pajamas. The rain danced on the porch.

And baby duck — holding onto taytay duck — looked right at the storm.

The storm looked back.

Baby duck did not run away.

"Hi, storm," he whispered.

The storm roared. Baby duck did not cry.

Taytay duck closed the door. They walked back to bed.

"You were BRAVE," said taytay duck.

"I was scared," said baby duck.

"Yes. And you were brave ANYWAY."

Baby duck thought about that all the way to sleep.

Being strong isn't not being scared.
Being strong is doing the important thing even when your heart goes DUB-DUB-DUB.

And the storm raged all night.
But baby duck slept all the way through.
