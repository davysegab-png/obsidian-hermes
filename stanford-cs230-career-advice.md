# Stanford CS230 — Career Advice in AI
*Andrew Ng + Laurence Moroney | Key notes from lecture*

---

## Andrew Ng's Points

**The best time ever to build with AI.**
Every 7 months, the complexity of tasks AI can handle doubles. Barrier to entry for powerful software has collapsed.

**Speed is the new currency.**
His "favorite tool" changes every 3-6 months. Being half a generation behind = significant productivity drop. Hyper-adaptive is the only option.

**The bottleneck shifted — from HOW to WHAT.**
Writing code got cheaper and faster. The real bottleneck now is deciding what to build. Engineers who can also talk to users, understand needs, and decide direction = most valuable assets.

**The Engineer + PM roles are collapsing.**
Traditional: 1 PM per 4-8 engineers. Now trending: 1:1 or no PM at all. The ability to code is no longer enough on its own.

**Optimize for the team, not the company.**
Your learning rate is predicted by the 5 people you interact with most. A smaller company with a brilliant team beats a "hot AI brand" where you're assigned to a backend Java payment team for a year.

---

## Laurence Moroney's Points

**"The Great Adjustment" (2024-2025)**
Post-pandemic over-hiring frenzy + market correction = entry-level positions feel scarce. Reality is harsh.

**Three Pillars of Success:**

1. **Understanding in depth**
   - Can't rely on high-level APIs alone
   - Need academic depth + knowing what's actually working in industry vs what's hype

2. **Business focus**
   - "Coolness for coolness sake" is dead
   - Companies are ruthlessly focused on the bottom line
   - Moroney: "Your output should be for the job you WANT, not the job you have"

3. **Job application strategy**
   - Don't prepare to answer brain teasers
   - Build something on their cloud infrastructure BEFORE the interview
   - He landed Google by building a stock prediction app on Google Cloud before his interview

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Product sense > pure coding.** The bottleneck moved from building to deciding what to build.
- **Speed + adaptability = currency.** Tools change fast. Falling behind half a generation is costly.
- **Build before the interview.** Moroney's Google play: build on their infra first.
- **Team > brand name.** A supportive brilliant team accelerates faster than prestige.
- **Depth + business focus = survival.** Just knowing APIs isn't enough. Understand why something matters to a business.
