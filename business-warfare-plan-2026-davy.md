## OPTION 1 — "THE MIRAGE CLOSE"

**One-sentence summary:**
Target local service businesses (real estate agents, contractors, coaches), pitch them a FREE 1-week pilot automation in exchange for a testimonial and referral — then convert the pilot into a $1,500-$3k/mo retainer.

**Why this fits you personally:**
You enjoy building. You said "it works now" is your favorite part. This lets you BUILD FIRST — no endless planning, no outreach without proof. The free pilot removes price objections entirely. You have nothing to lose and a case study to gain. This aligns with your fun-first energy: show up, build something cool, let results speak.

**Deep research summary:**
The #1 mistake AI agency newbies make is building the perfect system before getting a single paying client. The operators doing $10k+/mo right now — the $25k/mo Reddit AMA guy, dashboardlim, the kuga.ai roadmap — all started with outreach-first or even free-work-first. The "mirage close" is a known playbook: give them a taste, prove the value, then close. Real estate agents in particular are described as "fast decision cycle, $500-$6k per build, will pay" (abhyashsuchi.in). Home service contractors have massive manual admin waste and competitive pressure to adopt AI. The $25k/mo operator said: "i definitely did outreach first. built nothing until i had someone who actually wanted to pay for it. too many people build the perfect system and then try to sell it later — backwards."

**30-60 day execution plan:**

### Week 1 — NICHE + PROOF OF CONCEPT
**Deliverable:** 1 working demo automation you can show (not a generic demo — something with a specific use case)
- Davy owns: pick 1 niche (recommend real estate agents OR home service contractors), build 1 demo automation relevant to that niche
- Hermes owns: research 20 target businesses in that niche in Davy's area/region, build outreach message templates, identify decision trigger language

### Week 2 — OUTREACH SPRINT
**Deliverable:** 15-20 outreach messages sent (DM or email, not cold email — personal, specific)
- Davy owns: send the messages personally, track responses in a simple spreadsheet
- Hermes owns: daily follow-up message drafts, response analysis, rewrite underperforming messages

### Week 3 — PILOT DELIVERY
**Deliverable:** 1 pilot running live for 1 client
- Davy owns: build the pilot (you love this part), deliver it, document the results with screenshots/metrics
- Hermes owns: pilot success metrics doc, testimonial quote drafted for client to approve, referral request script

### Week 4 — THE CLOSE + REFERERAL HARVEST
**Deliverable:** Signed retainer ($1,500-$3k/mo) + 1 referral intro
- Davy owns: the conversation, close the retainer, ask for referral by name
- Hermes owns: retainer contract template, referral intro email template, pricing guide ($1,500/mo = $18k/year per client — 2 clients = $36k/year)

### Weeks 5-8 — REPEAT WITH PROOF
**Deliverable:** Client #2 from referral, third client from warm outreach
- Davy owns: build, deliver, ask for referrals
- Hermes owns: outreach to referral warm leads, case study one-pager for client #1 to share

**Team division of labor:**
- Davy = builder, closer, deliverer
- Hermes = researcher, copywriter, strategist, tracker

**Resources needed:**
- n8n (free tier is fine to start)
- Telegram or email for outreach
- Simple spreadsheet for tracking
- Google/My Business to find local businesses
- Zero budget required

**Risks & mitigation:**
- RISK: Client doesn't convert to paying. MITIGATION: You have a live case study either way. Worst case: you now have proof for next outreach. Don't do free work for more than 2 clients — if you haven't converted by client #2, something's wrong with the pitch, not the product.
- RISK: You spend all week building and no one responds to outreach. MITIGATION: The outreach is Hermes's job to optimize. If 0 responses by day 3, we rewrite the entire message. Not the strategy — the message.
- RISK: You're building the wrong thing. MITIGATION: Pick 1 niche only. Real estate OR home services. Not both. The mistake is trying to be everything to everyone.

**Success metrics / KPIs:**
- Week 2: 15+ outreach messages sent, 3+ responses
- Week 3: 1 pilot live
- Week 4: $1,500-$3k/mo retainer signed

- KPI: Cost to acquire a paying client (should be under 10 hours of outreach)

**How this ladders into $10k/mo by 2026:**

- 3 clients @ $2,500/mo = $7.5k/mo
- 4 clients @ $2,500/mo + 1 project build ($3k) = $13k/mo
- Target: 4 retainers by end of Q2 2026 = $10k/mo run rate
- 1 project build in between = bonus

## OPTION 2 — "THE UPWORK GRIND"

**One-sentence summary:**
Create a focused Upwork profile as an "AI Workflow Automation Specialist" targeting small businesses, win 2-3 first projects at $500-$1k to build reviews, then immediately raise prices and target SMBs willing to pay $2k-$5k.

**Why this fits you personally:**
No network is actually fine here — Upwork is pure meritocracy. Multiple operators confirmed this path: $40k in 6 months from scratch on Upwork with no code (r/gohighlevel). You get to skip the "find a client" problem entirely — the marketplace brings them to you. You're competitive and like seeing results fast. Upwork delivers fast feedback loops. The downside: it takes time to build profile authority, and you'll be competing on price early.

**Deep research summary:**
Upwork is CONFIRMED as a viable first-client channel. r/gohighlevel: "First client on Upwork, then second, then more. Total: $40,000 in 6 months — no code, no team." The key is positioning: don't compete as "AI automation" — compete as "I automate [specific problem] for [specific industry]." The $25k/mo operator built nothing until someone paid. Upwork lets you do the reverse — list what you do, let buyers come. The grind part is real: first $500-$1k projects are tuition. But kuga.ai's roadmap shows hitting £600 MRR (~$750/mo) with 3 clients at £199/mo from testing in days 8-30. You won't get rich on Upwork alone, but you WILL get real clients, real reviews, and real proof.

**30-60 day execution plan:**

### Week 1 — PROFILE + POSITIONING
**Deliverable:** Complete Upwork profile live with portfolio
- Davy owns: set up profile, select 1 niche focus, write service description (outcome-focused language)
- Hermes owns: profile optimization (headline, overview, SEO keywords), portfolio entry descriptions, competitor analysis of top-rated AI automation freelancers

### Week 2 — FIRST 3 PROPOSALS
**Deliverable:** 3 proposals sent to relevant jobs
- Davy owns: approve and send proposals
- Hermes owns: find matching jobs daily, write customized proposal drafts, optimize for AI-related job alerts

### Week 3 — DELIVERY SPRINT
**Deliverable:** Win 1 project, start delivery
- Davy owns: if project won, build it. If not, send 5 more proposals + optimize approach
- Hermes owns: proposal performance tracking, follow-up messages, "why you didn't win" analysis if outbid

### Week 4 — CLOSE + REVIEW HARVEST
**Deliverable:** Project complete, 5-star review posted
- Davy owns: deliver excellent work, request the review
- Hermes owns: review response strategy, next project targeting (higher price tier)

### Weeks 5-8 — RAISE PRICES + REPEAT
**Deliverable:** 2 more clients at $1,500-$2k+ each
- Davy owns: delivery, relationship management
- Hermes owns: outreach to past clients for repeat work, upsell template ($500/mo retainer = $6k/year vs one-time project)

**Team division of labor:**
- Davy = builder, deliverer, proposal sender
- Hermes = profile optimizer, job finder, proposal writer, tracker

**Resources needed:**
- Upwork profile (free to create)
- n8n (free tier)
- Maybe: small Upwork "connects" budget ($10-$20 to stand out)
- Hermes research support for proposal writing

**Risks & mitigation:**
- RISK: Upwork takes 1-2 months to build momentum. MITIGATION: Don't do this as your ONLY channel. Run Option 1 and Option 2 simultaneously — Upwork is passive inbound while you do outreach.
- RISK: You get trapped in low-ball projects ($100-$300). MITIGATION: After first 2 reviews, immediately raise prices to $1k minimum per project. Never accept less. The goal is reviews, not income — but you still need to eat.
- RISK: Competition from overseas operators. MITIGATION: Position on OUTCOMES, not price. "I saved a real estate agent 10 hours/week" is a better profile than "I set up n8n automations."

**Success metrics / KPIs:**
- Week 2: 3+ proposals sent
- Week 4: 1 project won, delivery started
- Week 8: 2-3 completed projects, 3+ reviews, $2k+ earned
- KPI: Upwork profile rating above 4.9

**How this ladders into $10k/mo by 2026:**
- Month 2: $1,500-$2k from Upwork projects
- Month 3: $2.5k/mo (1 retainer + project work)
- Month 4: $4k/mo (2 retainers + project work)
- Month 5+: $7-10k/mo from 3-4 active retainers
- Upwork becomes the "proof engine" — every client you deliver well on becomes a case study for your outreach (Option 1)

## OPTION 3 — "THE PARTNERSHIP PLAY"

**One-sentence summary:**
Find 3-5 complementary solo service providers (web designers, marketing consultants, accountants, business coaches) who already serve small businesses and propose a commission-based referral partnership — they refer AI automation clients to you, you pay 15-20% of the first project or 1 month retainer.

**Why this fits you personally:**
You have ZERO network in business. But everyone who serves small businesses HAS a network. A web designer who built 50 local business websites knows 50 business owners. An accountant sees which businesses are growing and have budget. A business coach is already in conversation about growth. You're not asking them to do your job — you're giving them a passive income stream by referring their clients to you. And the baby coming means you need leverage — you can't spend 40 hours/week doing outreach AND building AND closing.

**Deep research summary:**
Multiple sources confirm partnership/channel models work. r/aiagents: "Partnership distribution tactic: partner with niche leaders who don't know AI, use their distribution network for win-win." The $10k/mo IndieHackers operator: "biggest unlock was stopping guessing, started deploying inside own existing clients" — meaning they used their existing business relationships as the testing ground. The revenue share model (shared savings) is confirmed working — clients like it because no upfront risk, operators like it because recurring revenue. The key insight from r/smallbusiness: "if an automation is making or saving them 10-20k a month then charging 1-3k setup and 500-1500/mo retainer is a steal." You don't need to find the end buyer — you need to find someone who already has the end buyer's ear.

**30-60 day execution plan:**

### Week 1 — FIND PARTNERS
**Deliverable:** Identify 10 potential partners (web designers, coaches, accountants, marketing freelancers in your area/online)
- Davy owns: reach out to anyone you know — former coworkers, friends, acquaintances who work in adjacent fields
- Hermes owns: LinkedIn/X search for solo service providers who serve SMBs, compile list with contact info and what they offer

### Week 2 — OUTREACH TO PARTNERS
**Deliverable:** 10 personalized partnership intro messages sent
- Davy owns: send messages, follow up, schedule discovery calls
- Hermes owns: write partnership pitch message, one-pager (what you do, who you serve, what they earn), referral agreement draft

### Week 3 — PARTNERSHIP CALLS
**Deliverable:** 3-5 discovery calls, 2 signed partnership agreements
- Davy owns: the calls (30 min each — what problems do their clients face? what does their client trust them on?)
- Hermes owns: call prep questions, post-call summary, referral tracker setup

### Week 4 — FIRST REFERRALS + PILOT
**Deliverable:** 1 referral from a partner, first pilot with that referral
- Davy owns: if a referral comes, treat them like gold — build the pilot, deliver fast
- Hermes owns: thank-you note to referring partner, commission tracking, payment process

### Weeks 5-8 — FEED THE PIPELINE
**Deliverable:** 3+ referrals in pipeline, 1 more paying client converted
- Davy owns: deliver for referred clients, ask for referrals from the referred clients (creating a second layer)
- Hermes owns: partner communication cadence (weekly check-in message to each partner), new partner prospecting

**Team division of labor:**
- Davy = relationship owner, builder, deliverer
- Hermes = researcher, copywriter, agreement drafter, tracker, comms manager to partners

**Resources needed:**
- LinkedIn (free) for prospecting partners
- Simple referral agreement (Hermes can draft)
- Commission budget: 15-20% of first project (comes from project revenue, not extra cost to client)
- No tools required beyond what you already have

**Risks & mitigation:**
- RISK: Partners don't refer anyone. MITIGATION: The first 2-3 partners probably won't send referrals immediately — they're waiting to see if you're real. Deliver 1 pilot for a partner's client = instant credibility. If no referrals after 3 weeks, add more partners.
- RISK: Commission structure eats your margin. MITIGATION: Only pay commission on the FIRST project, not ongoing retainers. Or pay 1 month retainer as commission. After that, the client is yours.
- RISK: You don't know anyone. MITIGATION: This is the hardest part of this option. But even reaching out to web designers on LinkedIn cold is lower friction than reaching the end SMB client directly. You're going through someone they already trust.

**Success metrics / KPIs:**
- Week 2: 10 partnership messages sent
- Week 3: 3+ discovery calls, 2 signed partners
- Week 4: 1 referral received, 1 pilot started
- Week 8: 2 paying clients from referrals, 3 active partners
- KPI: Partner conversion rate (should be 20-30% of active partners sending at least 1 referral within 30 days)

**How this ladders into $10k/mo by 2026:**
- 5 active partners sending 1 referral each/month = 5 new leads/month
- If 50% convert: 2-3 new clients/month
- 3 active clients @ $2.5k/mo = $7.5k/mo
- 5 partners each sending 1 referral/month at $2k project = $10k/mo in project fees
- This option is the LONG GAME — it compounds over 6-12 months, not 30 days
